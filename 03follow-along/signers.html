Who Were the Signers

Famous Singers

few names stand out due to their massive historical impact or unique circumstances regarding the document itself.

John Hancock
The President of the Continental Congress and the first to sign. His large, flamboyant signature made his name a synonym for "signature" in American English.
Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the Declaration and a member of the Committee of Five. He later served as the third U.S. President.
Benjamin Franklin
The oldest signer at age 70 and a world-renowned scientist and diplomat. He is one of the few founders to sign both the Declaration and the Constitution.
John Adams
A fierce advocate for independence and a member of the drafting committee. He later became the second U.S. President.
Samuel Adams
A political organizer and leader of the Sons of Liberty. He was a key figure in early resistance movements like the Boston Tea Party.
Edward Rutledge
The youngest person to sign the Declaration of Independence at the age of 26.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The longest-lived and last surviving signer, who passed away in 1832 at the age of 95.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four of the great state papers of the United States, including the Constitution and the Declaration.
Button Gwinnett
A representative from Georgia whose signature is considered the rarest and most valuable among collectors of the signers.

Some famous patriots like George Washington and Alexander Hamilton did not sign the Declaration because they were away serving in the military or were not members of the Continental Congress at the time.
