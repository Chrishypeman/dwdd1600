Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship

Digital citizenship is the responsible, ethical, and safe use of technology to engage in online communities, encompassing behaviors like respecting others

What is Digital Law?

Digital law (or cyber law) is the body of law regulating activities and relationships in the digital world, covering legal rights and restrictions for using technology and the internet

What is AUP?

AUP most commonly stands for Acceptable Use Policy, a set of rules defining how individuals can use an organization's technology

What is Plagiarsim?

Plagiarism is presenting someone else's words, ideas, or work as your own without giving proper credit, essentially committing intellectual theft, whether intentionally or accidentally

It involves failing to cite the original source. It covers using text, concepts, artwork, or digital content from published or unpublished sources

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any deceptive behavior in educational settings that provides an unfair advantage

including plagiarism, cheating, fabrication, and unauthorized collaboration, essentially claiming work or credit that isn't your own

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

Rules for AI-generated content involve mandatory disclosure, ensuring human authorship for copyright
